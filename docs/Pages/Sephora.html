Sephora Product and Review Analysis

Objectives

  • Understand customer sentiment by analyzing reviews using machine learning to classify them as positive, negative, or neutral.
  • Identify key factors shaping opinions, such as brand perception, product effectiveness across skin tones, and the link between price and satisfaction.
  • Provide Sephora with actionable insights to improve product offerings, marketing strategies, and customer experience.
  • Build a personalized recommendation system that suggests relevant skincare and beauty products based on user inputs (skin type, category, and budget).
  • Use TF-IDF and KNN models to deliver accurate, data-driven product suggestions that reduce decision fatigue and enhance satisfaction.

    • Methodology

  • Conducted EDA to uncover trends in sentiment by brand, price, product type, and skin type.
  • Cleaned and preprocessed review data through tokenization, stopword removal, and stemming.
  • Filtered out low-rated products and removed duplicates to ensure data quality.
  • Engineered features by combining key product attributes for improved similarity detection.
  • Applied TF-IDF Vectorization and KNN with cosine similarity to build the recommendation engine.
  • Used sentiment analysis to classify reviews and extract themes like hydration, fit, and effectiveness.
  • Evaluated model performance using a train-test split and selected the best-performing models based on accuracy and relevance.

    • Data Overview

    Data Source
    • Kaggle: Sephora Dataset

    The Sephora Products and Skincare Reviews dataset from Kaggle includes detailed information on beauty and skincare products sold at Sephora.

    Key Data Points

    Product name, brand name, price (in USD), skin type, category classifications, user reviews, and ratings

    Data Cleaning

  • Randomized subset of 100,000 data points from the original 1M+ dataset.
  • Dropped missing values and duplicates.
  • Recommendation System: Filtered ratings with 4 stars and above.

    • Sentiment Analysis by Reviews

    • Negative reviews are often longer and more detailed, suggesting that dissatisfied customers provide more in-depth feedback.
    • Brands like CLINIQUE and Youth To The People receive higher positive sentiment, indicating an opportunity to replicate their success factors across other brands.
    Sentiment Analysis by Skin Type

    • Dry Skin: Mixed reviews with frequent dryness complaints. The brand could explore a more hydrating formula to address these concerns.
    • Oily Skin: Mostly positive reviews. The lightweight, non-greasy texture is a strong selling point.
    Sentiment Analysis by Brands

    • Price ≠ Positive Sentiment: La Mer’s mixed reviews show that luxury doesn’t guarantee satisfaction.
    • Brand Loyalty Opportunity: Bobbi Brown and other top-rated brands can leverage positive sentiment to boost loyalty and refine marketing strategies.
    Sentiment Analysis by Price

    • Luxury brands: Should improve customer satisfaction to justify premium pricing, as high price doesn’t always translate to high sentiment (e.g., La Mer).
    • Top-rated brands: Brands like Bobbi Brown and Shiseido can leverage strong sentiment to boost loyalty and explore premium product lines.

    Recommendation System

    Data Processing & Feature Engineering (TF-IDF Vectorization)

    • Combines important text features: brand_name, skin_type, secondary_category
    • Uses TF-IDF Vectorizer to convert text data into numerical vectors
    • Helps the model understand text-based similarities between products

    Finding Similar Products using KNN

    • Uses K-Nearest Neighbors (KNN) with cosine similarity
    • Finds the most similar products based on the vectorized features
    • Computes top 5 nearest neighbors for each product

    Recommendation Logic

    • Filters products based on personalized inputs such as skin_type, max_price, and product_category
    • Leverages KNN similarity results to suggest closely matched alternatives
    • Eliminates duplicate entries and ranks products by price (highest to lowest)
    • Returns the top 5 most relevant and affordable product recommendations

    Conclusion

    Recommendation System

    • Filters products based on individual preferences: skin type, budget, and preferred product category
    • Suggests top 5 alternatives using KNN-based similarity scoring
    • Enhances the shopping experience with personalized, data-backed recommendations

    Sentiment Analysis

    • Positive sentiment is highest among oily skin users, indicating strong product satisfaction within this group
    • Luxury pricing does not necessarily lead to higher satisfaction — effectiveness matters more than cost
    • Brands should enhance formulations for dry skin users to address frequent complaints
    • Top-performing brands can reinforce loyalty and brand equity through consistent product quality and customer engagement
